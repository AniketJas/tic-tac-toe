Tic-tac-toe is a classic game played on a 3x3 grid. Two players, usually represented as "X" and "O," take turns marking empty squares. The objective is to form a line of three of your symbols horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.


Here are the basic rules:

  1. Players take turns placing their symbol in an empty square on the grid.
  2. The game starts with an empty grid. X typically goes first, followed by O, alternating until the game ends.
  3. A player wins if they successfully create a line of three of their symbols in any direction.
  4. If all squares are filled without a winner, the game ends in a draw.
  5. Players cannot overwrite each other's symbols once placed.
  6. Once a player achieves victory or a draw, the game ends.

Tic-tac-toe is a simple yet strategic game, often played for entertainment and as a teaching tool for basic game theory.
